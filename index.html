和
The Core Mental Model
50-70 MIN
Why Japan is the way it is

Japan's entire social fabric flows from wa (和): group harmony. Layer on tatemae vs honne (public face vs private feelings), shoganai (radical acceptance of what can't change), and omotenashi (hospitality that anticipates needs before they're expressed). You're not learning trivia. You're installing a lens.

Society in Practice
70-90 MIN
How those values show up in daily life

Now apply the lens. Japan's paradoxes only make sense once you know wa. The country that invented the bullet train also built izakayas where salarymen silently drink alone. The society that banned street littering has no public bins. These aren't bugs. They're the system working exactly as intended.

Final discussion: 30-60 min
The Case Study Prompt

Prepare independently, then talk. The best discussions end with a real decision you genuinely disagree on.

You're planning Day 4 in Tokyo and Day 8 in Kyoto. Using what you've studied — wa, omotenashi, ma, tatemae/honne — pitch one experience per city that a standard guidebook would miss. Explain what cultural insight makes it worth doing over the obvious choice.

Then defend or challenge this: "As a Western tourist in Japan, you can only ever access a curated performance of Japanese culture. Never the real thing."

01

Pick a side on the debate. Even if it's not your genuine view. Argue it seriously.

02

Bring one thing from your research that genuinely surprised or delighted you.

03

End with a real decision: where are you actually going, and why that over the alternative.
